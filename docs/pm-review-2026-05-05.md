# PM Review — Release Plans & PRD
**Reviewed against:** `research/MARKET-RESEARCH.md`, `research/USER-RESEARCH.md`
**Date:** 2026-05-05
**Reviewer:** Product

---

## Summary Verdict

The core strategy is sound — mid-market positioning, <45-minute time-to-value, upload-first flow. But there are **four structural problems** that could cause the Beta to fail its own success criteria, and several assumptions in the PRD that are now stale given what the research surfaced.

---

## 1. BUG-006 is in the wrong milestone — and it breaks the Beta's success criteria

**The contradiction:** The PRD lists "Clause-level audit trail — clickable, with clause text visible on hover or tap" as **P0, must ship in V1**. The Beta plan lists BUG-006 (clause PDF viewer) as **deferred to GA**. The user research calls it a **hard auditor acceptance blocker**.

The Beta's own success criterion is: *"≥1 report per participating account submitted to and accepted by an external auditor without revision."* If auditors won't accept reports without a clause viewer, this criterion is structurally impossible to meet with BUG-006 deferred.

**Recommendation:** Either (a) pull BUG-006 into Beta entry criteria and accept the schedule risk, or (b) formally downgrade the Beta success criterion to "auditor receives and reviews the report" (not full acceptance). You can't have it both ways — defer BUG-006 and also require auditor sign-off in Beta.

---

## 2. Event tracking must be a Beta entry criterion, not just a GA criterion

The Beta plan measures time-to-report via "instrumented event log (upload timestamp → export timestamp)." But the PRD explicitly flags "no event tracking instrumentation" as a **P0 metric collection gap**. The GA plan lists event tracking wiring as due July 7 — *after Beta ends on July 14*.

If event tracking isn't in place during Beta, you cannot validate your primary success criterion quantitatively. You'll be relying on self-reported times from participants, which is unreliable.

**Recommendation:** Add event tracking to Beta entry criteria with a May 25 deadline, the same window as the other P0 gates.

---

## 3. "Primary tool, not parallel" is too aggressive for Beta week 1

The Beta plan asks participants to run their real compliance cycle using LegalGraph as their **primary** tool. User research shows a 13.5% AI tool adoption rate in legal (Deloitte 2025), significant trust gaps around AI accuracy, and Jennifer's specific concern about career risk if a report is wrong.

Asking users to drop their Excel fallback from day one means a single extraction failure or webhook downtime incident could compromise their actual audit submission.

**Recommendation:** Weeks 2–3 run in parallel (LegalGraph + their existing process). Weeks 4–5 they submit the LegalGraph output if they're confident. Auditor acceptance can still be validated in week 5. This also yields direct time-comparison data (LegalGraph vs. manual), which is more compelling evidence than self-reported estimates.

---

## 4. Three open questions in the PRD are past-due and blocking GA features

| Question | PRD deadline | Impact |
|----------|-------------|--------|
| Discount rate — IBR default or manual input? | April 7 | Affects every contract without a rate; Beta plan says "one-pager workaround" — not a product decision |
| IFRS 16 vs ASC 842 priority | April 5 | ASC 842 is listed as P1 in GA plan but nobody confirmed the standard split |
| Auditor export format — PDF, Excel, or structured data? | April 10 | GA plan ships PDF export but this was never validated with auditors |

The discount rate question is especially urgent — market research rates it as a **High-severity** pain point and user research flags it as a consistent field where AI extraction fails. Without a product decision, the Beta will produce High flags on nearly every contract with no clear resolution path.

**Recommendation:** Resolve all three during Beta participant recruitment calls, before Beta starts.

---

## 5. GA P1 scope is too ambitious

The GA plan ships **5 significant P1 features simultaneously**: ASC 842, amendment tracking, bulk report generation, email delivery, and the Audit Trail tab. None of these will have been tested in Beta. If any one breaks post-GA, it's a Wave 2/3 incident with existing enterprise accounts watching.

**Recommendation:** Ship ASC 842 at GA (market-table-stakes per research). Defer amendment tracking, bulk generation, email delivery, and the Audit Trail tab to GA+1. Use the freed-up scope to close BUG-006 before Beta.

---

## 6. "Customers will upload all leases" has no mitigation strategy

The PRD rates this as **Low confidence** — but neither the Beta nor GA plan addresses what happens if customers only upload 1–2 leases. The North Star metric requires full portfolio adoption to be meaningful. User research showed Jennifer's specific concern: *"Where does our contract data go?"*

**Recommendation:** Frame Beta onboarding as "start with your most complex lease" rather than "upload everything." Measure portfolio depth per account in Beta as a leading indicator of GA adoption risk.

---

## 7. Pricing timeline creates a Beta commitment gap

The GA plan offers Beta participants "3 months free" but pricing is listed as TBD. The Beta NDA and DPA (due May 28) will likely need to reference commercial terms. If pricing isn't set before invitations go out June 2, participants sign a legal agreement without knowing what they're committing to at month 4.

**Recommendation:** Set at minimum the pricing tier structure (per-lease-count bands) before May 28.

---

## What's Working Well

- **Report gate** (High flags block export) is a genuine differentiator — no competitor enforces this structurally.
- **Beta exclusion of regulated sectors** based on RAI score (58/112) is the right call per user research.
- **Staged GA rollout** (4 waves) with a Wave 1→2 pause condition is well-designed.
- **Auditor explainer PDF on every export** directly addresses the AI trust gap the research surfaced.
- **Beta NPS ≥40 graduation gate** is a high bar and should stay high — these are reference customers.
