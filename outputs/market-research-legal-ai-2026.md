# Market Research: AI-Powered Contract Review & Legal AI Market
**Date**: May 8, 2026
**Researcher**: Senior PM, LegalGraph

---

## Executive Summary

The AI-powered contract review market is in a high-growth phase, with the AI contract analysis tools segment expanding from $3.32B in 2025 to $4.30B in 2026 at a 29.6% CAGR — outpacing the broader CLM market (11–13% CAGR). Legal tech funding hit a record $4.3B in 2025 (+54% YoY), signaling strong investor conviction that AI will fundamentally reshape legal operations.

The competitive landscape has shifted materially since LegalGraph's last analysis. Evisort was acquired by Workday ($310M, Oct 2024), effectively removing it as a standalone mid-market competitor and bundling it into enterprise ERP workflows. Ironclad crossed $196M ARR in 2025 (34% YoY growth), cementing Tier 1 status. Harvey raised a $300M Series E, threatening to commoditize general-purpose legal AI. Luminance is building strong European presence with proprietary "Legal-Grade AI."

**For LegalGraph, the window to own mid-market AI contract review is open but narrowing.** The key opportunity: Evisort's Workday integration alienates buyers who want a standalone review tool; Ironclad remains overpriced for mid-market; and new entrants (Harvey, Spellbook) are general-purpose tools without playbook enforcement depth. LegalGraph's $45k/year price point, 2-week setup, and playbook customization remain strong differentiation — but accuracy validation and integration breadth must accelerate in 2026.

## Competitive Analysis

### Competitor 1: Ironclad

- **Overview:** Series E ($333M raised), $196M ARR in 2025 (34% YoY), Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader and Forrester Wave Leader for CLM (2025). CEO transitioning to Executive Chairman role.
- **Strengths:** Full CLM suite, brand recognition, Salesforce/DocuSign ecosystem, white-glove enterprise support.
- **Weaknesses:** $100k–$300k/year pricing, 6–12 month implementation, overkill for mid-market, AI is an add-on not core.
- **Market position:** Tier 1 enterprise leader; upmarket from LegalGraph's sweet spot.
- **Pricing:** $100k–$300k/year + $50–150k implementation.
- **Threat to LegalGraph:** Low in mid-market; growing threat if they launch a simplified mid-market tier.

---

### Competitor 2: Harvey

- **Overview:** General-purpose legal AI platform. Raised $300M Series E in early 2025. Targets law firms and large in-house teams with broad AI capabilities (contracts, litigation, compliance, due diligence).
- **Strengths:** Massive funding, broad applicability, strong brand momentum, used by major law firms (A&O Shearman, PwC Legal).
- **Weaknesses:** Not specialized for contract review; no playbook enforcement; accuracy on niche clause types unproven; no mid-market pricing.
- **Market position:** Emerging Tier 1 for general legal AI, not yet a direct CLM/review competitor.
- **Pricing:** Enterprise/custom, not publicly disclosed.
- **Threat to LegalGraph:** Medium — if Harvey launches a contract review module with playbook support, it could compete in mid-market with brand advantage.

---

### Competitor 3: Evisort (Workday)

- **Overview:** Acquired by Workday for ~$310M (Oct 2024). Fully integrated into Workday by March 2025. AI-native CLM trained on 11M+ contracts. No longer a standalone product.
- **Strengths:** Deep Workday ecosystem integration, strong post-signature analytics, enterprise-grade AI.
- **Weaknesses:** Now bundled with Workday — only accessible to Workday customers. Not a standalone purchase. Loses relevance for non-Workday accounts. Pre-signature review secondary to repository analysis.
- **Market position:** Removed from standalone mid-market consideration; now enterprise only via Workday.
- **Pricing:** Bundled into Workday enterprise contracts.
- **Threat to LegalGraph:** **Reduced** — Workday acquisition is a net positive for LegalGraph. Mid-market buyers who evaluated Evisort now need an alternative.

---

### Competitor 4: Luminance

- **Overview:** UK-based legal AI with proprietary "Legal-Grade™ AI" trained specifically on legal documents. Strong European growth (6x ARR growth). Targets law firms and corporate legal.
- **Strengths:** Proprietary legal AI (not GPT wrapper), strong EU market presence, broader legal workflows (review, due diligence, drafting), strong privacy posture for GDPR markets.
- **Weaknesses:** Less known in US mid-market, primarily law-firm focused, higher price point.
- **Market position:** Tier 2 in US; Tier 1 in European legal AI.
- **Pricing:** Not publicly disclosed; estimated $50k–$120k/year.
- **Threat to LegalGraph:** Medium — low US threat today; higher if LegalGraph expands to UK/EU.

---

### Competitor 5: Spellbook

- **Overview:** Contract drafting and review tool embedded in Microsoft Word. Uses LLMs to suggest clause alternatives, flag risks, and redline contracts. Targets transactional lawyers.
- **Strengths:** Zero-friction adoption (lives in Word), fast setup, accessible pricing, strong lawyer UX.
- **Weaknesses:** No playbook enforcement, no portfolio analytics, no workflow/approval routing, point solution.
- **Market position:** SMB/solo practitioner + small in-house teams; below LegalGraph's enterprise target.
- **Pricing:** ~$149–$299/user/month (SMB-friendly).
- **Threat to LegalGraph:** Low for enterprise targets; could compete for smaller teams or users who avoid adopting a new platform.

## Market Opportunities

### 1. Evisort Displacement — Workday Orphans
- **Market gap:** Evisort customers or evaluators who are NOT Workday customers now need an alternative standalone contract review + AI platform.
- **Potential impact:** Estimated 200–500 mid-market accounts actively evaluating alternatives post-acquisition. Average deal size $60–100k.
- **Requirements:** Dedicated outbound motion targeting former Evisort prospects; competitive migration materials; post-signature analytics roadmap to match key Evisort features.

### 2. Mid-Market AI Upgrade Cycle
- **Market gap:** AI adoption in corporate legal doubled to 52% in 2025 but most teams are using generic tools (ChatGPT, Copilot) not purpose-built playbook-enforcing review platforms. 69% now using AI but only ~20% at "AI maturity."
- **Potential impact:** Large volume of mid-market legal teams in "active evaluation" stage for a purpose-built tool. LegalGraph's 2-week onboarding and $45k price is well-positioned for first-time buyers.
- **Requirements:** Strong activation flow (55% → 70% target), ROI calculator front and center, free trial or pilot program.

### 3. EU AI Act Compliance Urgency (EU Expansion)
- **Market gap:** August 2026 brings full enforcement of EU AI Act for high-risk AI systems. Legal AI used in contract decisions qualifies. EU buyers need vendors with explainability, audit logs, and data residency.
- **Potential impact:** Opens UK/EU mid-market (est. 2x US SAM size). LegalGraph has EU West AWS region and audit logs already.
- **Requirements:** GDPR DPA, EU data residency guarantee, SOC 2 Type II (have) + ISO 27001, explainability report for AI decisions.

### 4. Law Firm Edition
- **Market gap:** Kira is the law firm default but has legacy UI and desktop-only architecture. Harvey is too broad. No modern cloud-native contract review tool is purpose-built for law firm transactional teams.
- **Potential impact:** 50-200 attorney firms pay $80k–$150k/year for Kira. LegalGraph at $45k with modern UX could displace.
- **Requirements:** Matter/client management, billing code tagging, firm-level playbooks per client, partner/associate permission tiers.
